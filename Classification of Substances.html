Classification of Matter

HOMOGENEOUS SUBSTANCES

  • Substances that have a uniform appearance throughout (only one phase).

    • Includes:

    • PURE SUBSTANCES - Made of only one type of particle.

      • Examples:

      • Pure water (H₂O)
      • Pure gold (Au)

    • HOMOGENEOUS MIXTURES - Made of two or more substances mixed together so that they appear as one.

      • Examples:

      • Salt dissolved in water
      • Milk

HETEROGENEOUS SUBSTANCES

  • Substances that do not have a uniform appearance throughout.
  • Components are not dissolved in the solvent.

    • Includes:

    • MECHANICAL MIXTURES - Can easily see the different parts.

      • Examples:

      • Cereal in milk
      • Pizza

    • SUSPENSIONS - Solid is dispersed for a short time but then settles due to gravity.

      • Examples:

      • Sand in water
      • dust in the air

SOLUTIONS

  • SOLVENT
    • Substance that does the dissolving.
    • Usually the larger quantity.
    • Can be a gas, liquid, or solid, but usually are liquids.

  • SOLUTE
    • Substance that gets dissolved.
    • Usually the smaller quantity.
    • Can be a gas, liquid, or solid.

Example: When you mix salt with water:

  • Solvent = water
  • Solute = salt
  • Solution = saltwater

AQUEOUS

  • A substance being dissolved in water → water is the solvent
  • Water is the universal solvent
  • Aqueous solution = water + solute

PRECIPITATE

  • A solid that is formed and is separated from the rest of the solution during a chemical reaction.
  • Example: Milk + vinegar = curdling of the milk, forming curds (cheese)
    • Precipitate: curds

PHASE CHANGE

  • Boiling point and condensation point happen at the same temperature, but the opposite happens:

    • Boiling: Liquid → Gas
    • Condensation: Gas → Liquid


  • Melting point and freezing point happen at the same temperature, but the opposite happens:

    • Melting: Solid → Liquid
    • Freezing: Liquid → Solid

Note: Notice how the graph isn't increasing or decreasing during the process of a phase change? This is because the temperature stays the same.
