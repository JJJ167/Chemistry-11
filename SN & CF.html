Scientific Notation

  • Scientific notation (St.N) is used to write a very large or very small number.
  • We use the powers of 10 to express the number.
Standard Notation Scientific Notation
0.005 5×10−3
83,000 8.3×104
0.0000002 2×10−7
300,000,000 3×10 8

Rules for writing large numbers in scientific notation:

Write 123,456,789 in scientific notation

  • All numbers have a decimal point. Find the decimal: 123,456,789.

  • Move the decimal point left until there is only one non-zero digit to the left of the decimal point:1.23456789

  • Count the number of spaces you moved the decimal point: 8 → exponent = 8

    • 1.23456789x108


Rules for writing large numbers in scientific notation:

Write 0.000000123 in scientific notation

  • Move the decimal point right until there is only one non-zero digit to the left of the decimal point: 00000001.23

  • Count the number of spaces you moved the decimal point: 7

  • Since the decimal was moved to the right, the exponent should be negative → exponent = -7

    • 1.23x10- 7

Unit Conversions

For unit conversions, we use conversion factors. A conversion factor is a fractional expression that helps convert one unit of measurement to another.

Every unit conversion follows these steps:

U = I x CF...etc. (more than one conversion factor may be needed sometimes)

U = Unknown amount and units (what you are usually trying to find)

I = Initial amount and units

CF = the CF that will connect the units of I with the units of U


Examples of CFs:

24 hours 1 day
and
1 min 60 sec
12 eggs 1 doz
and
1 doz 12 eggs

*Conversion factors can always be flipped*


Solving:

1. If you can bike 12 km in 1 hour, how far can you bike in 7 hours?

U = ? km

I = 7 hours

Cf = 1 doz 12 eggs


Equation should look like this:

km = 7 hours x 12 km 1 hour

→

km = 7 hours x 12 km 1 hour = 84 km

*The units of the numerator must cancel with the units of the denominator of the CF*





2. How many seconds are there in 2 days?

U = ? seconds

I = 2 days

Cfs =
24 hours 1 day
1 min 60 sec
60 sec 1 min


Equation should look like this:

seconds = 2 days x 24 hours 1 day x 60 mins 1 hour x 60 secs 1 min

→

seconds = 2 days x 24 hours 1 day x 60 mins 1 hour x 60 secs 1 min = 172,800 seconds
