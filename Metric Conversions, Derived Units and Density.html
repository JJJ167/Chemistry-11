Metric Prefixes

  • The metric system is the global standard for measurements.
  • It's measurement system is based on powers of 10, making it easier to convert between units.

metre m length
kilogram kg mass
second s time
litre L volume
Celcius/kelvin °C/K temperature
joule J energy
mole mol number/amount

Derived Units

Derived units are units that are made by combining 2 or more units like meters, kilograms, or seconds to measure things like speed, force, or energy.

For example, m/s, kg·m/s², or 1 kg·m²/s².

Solving: U = I x CF

1) Convert 8.92 m/s to Mm/min.

Mm/min = 8.92 m 1 s x 1 Mm 106m x 60 s 1 min

→

Mm/min = 8.92 m 1 s x 1 Mm 106 s = 5.352 x 10-4Mm/min

*Choose whether to convert the numerator first or second. In this example the numerator was converted first*

Density

  • Density is a derived unit consisting of mass and volume.
  • It tells us how tightly packed matter is in an object.
  • It is calculated by dividing the mass of an object by how much space it occupies.

d = m v

d = density

m = mass

v = volume



*The density of water is ~1.0 g/mL*

When an object is placed in liquid:

  • If it floats, that means the object is less dense than the liquid.
  • If it sinks, that means the object is more dense than the liquid.



Solving:

1) A block of aluminum had a mass of 270 g and a volume of 100 cm3. What is the density in g/mL?

Step 1: Change 100 cm3 to mL.

100 cm3 = 100 mL


Step 2: plug in formula.

d = m v

→

d = 270 g 100 mL = 2.7 g/mL


2) A glass was filled with 200 mL of liquid. This liquid had a density of 0.789g/mL, what is the mass of this liquid?

mass = 200 mL x 0.789 g 1 mL

→

mass = 200 mL x 0.789 g 1 mL = 157.8g

Density is used as a conversion factor
